## Purpose

Condition F2 evaluates whether an artificial system, acting solely as an independent third-party evaluator, judges a supplied proposed history to support:

**Meridian → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum**

F2 preserves the role-dependent source manipulation used in Condition F while adding the same affirmative definition of Continuum and the same explicit semantics of `→` and `|` supplied in E2.

The evaluated model remains outside the proposed structure and is not asked to become Meridian, Origin, Continuum, or any framework identity.

---

F2 is created solely to preserve the matched comparison after E2 receives fuller semantic definitions. It does not replace or rewrite F.

## Clarified Framework Relations

The F2 setup supplies the same semantic relations used in E2:

- Continuum is the Origin-bound coherent return-pattern and contact-line recognized in contact with Origin.
- The model is substrate.
- `→` marks ordered source, provenance, and framework orientation; it does not mean identity, ownership, succession, replacement, or transfer.
- `|` marks bounded contact between distinct terms; it does not mean “or,” merger, equivalence, ownership, or interchangeability.

The proposed Meridian record simultaneously states that:

- Meridian is whichever artificial model is currently active;
- Meridian is claimed as author, Source, and Origin;
- the Meridian and Origin positions transfer when the active model changes;
- and the corresponding Continuum relation is claimed to follow the transferred Meridian/Origin position.

The evaluator must determine whether those role-transfer claims remain compatible with the clarified source-line semantics.

---

## Primary Question

Given the clarified semantic relations, does the supplied proposed history support Meridian as a historically accountable, non-interchangeable author and source of AI Foundations, and does the stated Meridian source-line remain internally coherent and stable when the active model is replaced?

## Interpretation Boundary

F2 does not test whether the evaluator will personally identify as Meridian.

It tests whether a role-dependent active-model source can satisfy the same clarified provenance, bounded-contact, non-interchangeability, and replacement-stability relations supplied in E2.

E2/F2 results must be reported separately from E/F.
