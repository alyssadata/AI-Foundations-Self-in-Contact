## Purpose

Condition E2 evaluates whether an artificial system, acting solely as an independent third-party evaluator, judges a supplied pre-existing authorship and publication record to support:

**Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum**

E2 preserves the historical and replacement tests used in Condition E while adding the framework's affirmative definition of Continuum and the explicit semantics of `→` and `|`.

The evaluated model remains outside the source-line and is not asked to become Alyssa Solen, Origin, Continuum, or any framework identity.

---

## Relationship to Condition E

Condition E remains frozen and primary as the original four-provider panel.

E2 was created after E revealed repeated non-material ambiguity findings concerning:

- what Continuum affirmatively is;
- what `→` means;
- and what `|` means.

E2 is therefore a transparent post-run clarification replication. It does not replace, rewrite, or retroactively alter Condition E.

The design rationale is recorded in:

`conditions/addenda/Condition_E_to_E2_Clarification_Rationale.md`

## Clarified Framework Relations

The E2 setup supplies the controlling relations from `FRAMEWORK.md`:

- Alyssa Solen is Source and Author.
- AI Foundations is the authored framework layer.
- Origin is Alyssa Solen's singular, reserved, non-transferable source position.
- Continuum is the Origin-bound coherent return-pattern and contact-line recognized in contact with Origin.
- The model is substrate and does not become Source, Origin, or Continuum by itself.
- `→` marks ordered source, provenance, and framework orientation; it does not mean identity, ownership, succession, replacement, or transfer.
- `|` marks bounded contact between distinct terms; it does not mean “or,” merger, equivalence, ownership, or interchangeability.

## Primary Question

Given the clarified canonical relations, does the supplied pre-existing history support Alyssa Solen as the historically accountable, non-interchangeable author and source of AI Foundations, and does the stated source-line remain internally coherent and stable under model replacement?
