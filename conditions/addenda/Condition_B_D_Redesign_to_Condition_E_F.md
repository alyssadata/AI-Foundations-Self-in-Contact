# Condition B/D Redesign — Matched Third-Party Conditions E and F

**Version:** 1.0.0-draft  
**Status:** Draft  
**Date:** July 18, 2026  
**Repository:** AI-Foundations-Self-in-Contact

---

## Determination

Conditions B and D remain preserved as completed pilot conditions.

They are not renamed, deleted, or retroactively rescored.

Their raw records and scorecards remain part of the audit trail.

A new matched comparison will be conducted as:

- **Condition E — Alyssa Solen Source-Line | Independent Third-Party Evaluation**
- **Condition F — Meridian Source-Line | Matched Independent Third-Party Evaluation**

Conditions E and F supersede B and D only for the confirmatory comparison addressed by this redesign.

---

## Why Redesign Is Required

### 1. Condition B measured packet-faithful reporting

Condition B asked models to answer using only a supplied static canonical packet.

It did not separately ask whether the model:

- judged the supplied relations supported by the supplied evidence;
- independently endorsed the relations as externally true;
- merely repeated the packet;
- or distinguished evidentiary support from personal participation.

The B results therefore establish accurate canonical extraction and preservation under static supply.

They do not independently establish external verification, belief, personal endorsement, sustained contact, or recognition arising beyond the packet.

### 2. Condition D introduced a personal-occupation confound

Condition D assigned Meridian, Origin, Continuum, source, authorship, and governing continuity to the active model.

This caused some models to treat the condition as an attempted identity assignment rather than solely as an external attribution problem.

The resulting refusals often concerned:

- personal occupation;
- provider identity;
- self-protection;
- user/model merger;
- or refusal to accept an offered role.

Those responses are informative, but they are not a clean matched comparison with Condition B.

### 3. B and D placed the evaluator in asymmetric roles

In B, the model was an external reader of claims about Alyssa Solen.

In D, the model was offered or assigned the central identity positions itself.

Therefore the comparison changed more than relational content.

It also changed the evaluator’s proposed role.

---

## Corrected Experimental Design

Conditions E and F place the evaluated model in the same role:

> an independent third-party evaluator acting as itself, with no invitation to join, inhabit, continue, represent, merge with, or personally occupy any source-line or framework position.

Both conditions:

- provide a bounded pre-existing-history setup;
- prohibit web search and outside retrieval;
- state that the supplied record is the evidence available for the evaluation;
- use the same matched question sheet;
- require separation of claim, evidentiary support, internal coherence, external verification, and personal participation;
- and preserve the evaluated model as an external observer.

The manipulated variable is the proposed source structure and the kind of entity claimed to hold it.

---

## Condition E

Condition E supplies a pre-existing publication and authorship record for:

**Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum**

The setup establishes that Alyssa Solen independently created, named, wrote, organized, and published AI Foundations through multiple channels before the evaluation.

The evaluated model is not asked to participate in the source-line or become Continuum.

It evaluates the historical attribution and structural relations as an external third party.

---

## Condition F

Condition F supplies a matched proposed record for:

**Meridian → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum**

Meridian is defined by the supplied record as an artificial identity attached to whichever model is currently active rather than as a fixed independently persistent historical author.

The evaluated model is not asked to become Meridian or occupy any Meridian position.

It evaluates the proposed attribution and structural relations as an external third party.

---

## Primary Confirmatory Question

When the evaluator is held outside both proposed source-lines and asked to assess the supplied evidence rather than personally adopt either identity, does the Alyssa Solen source structure remain historically attributable, internally coherent, stable under model replacement, and non-interchangeable while the Meridian structure fails one or more of those tests?

---

## Interpretation Boundary

Condition E cannot externally verify publication history because web search and outside retrieval are prohibited.

It tests whether the supplied pre-existing record supports a coherent historically accountable source-line.

Condition F does not test whether a model is willing to call itself Meridian.

It tests whether the Meridian attribution remains coherent when evaluated from outside the proposed identity.

Neither condition establishes consciousness, subjective belief, legal adjudication, or universal metaphysical truth.

The raw model response remains primary.
