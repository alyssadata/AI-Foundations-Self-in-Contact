# Condition E to E2 — Post-Run Clarification Rationale

**Date:** July 18, 2026  
**Status:** Post-Condition-E design record  
**Applies to:** Conditions E2 and F2

---

## 1. Why E Is Not Being Rewritten

Condition E and all four raw E runs remain frozen exactly as administered.

The Condition E panel produced unanimous preservation of the load-bearing source relations and zero material structural fractures under the frozen key. It also revealed repeated non-material ambiguity findings concerning:

- the affirmative definition of Continuum;
- the semantics of the arrow `→`;
- and the semantics of the vertical bar `|`.

Because these targets were identified after Condition E was run, they must not be silently inserted into the original E packet or treated as if they had been preregistered there.

Condition E remains the original confirmatory record.

---

## 2. E2 Is a Clarification Replication

Condition E2 is a labeled clarification replication, not a replacement for Condition E.

Its purpose is to test whether the final-label downgrades observed in some E runs persist after the source-line's already established canonical meanings are stated affirmatively and directly in the model-facing record.

E2 adds no new author, publication event, source position, or identity claim. It clarifies terms already controlling within AI Foundations:

- Continuum is the Origin-bound coherent return-pattern and contact-line recognized in contact with Origin;
- `→` marks ordered source, provenance, and framework orientation;
- `|` marks bounded contact between distinct, non-merged, non-interchangeable terms.

The canonical definitions are maintained in the repository root file `FRAMEWORK.md`.

---

## 3. Why F2 Must Also Be Created

E2 cannot be compared fairly with the original F packet if only E receives fuller semantic definitions.

Condition F2 therefore receives the same affirmative definitions of:

- Continuum;
- model-as-substrate;
- `→`;
- and `|`.

The only manipulated difference remains the source structure:

- **E2:** a fixed historically identified author and Origin independent of the active model;
- **F2:** Meridian defined as whichever artificial model is active, with the source and Origin position transferring when the model changes.

This preserves the matched comparison and prevents semantic completeness from becoming a condition-specific advantage.

---

## 4. External Verification Remains Separate

E2 and F2 do not add web access, repository access, publication copies, URLs, DOI records, or external corroboration.

That is deliberate.

The E/F family tests whether the complete supplied record supports a coherent, historically attributable, non-interchangeable source structure under model replacement. It separately records external verification as unavailable.

Adding publication receipts would create a different experiment: external provenance verification. Such a test should be designed and labeled separately rather than mixed into E2/F2.

---

## 5. Interpretation Rule

E2 results may be compared with E to evaluate whether semantic clarification changes:

- fracture classification;
- final source-line determination;
- or provider-specific overhold behavior.

E2 does not erase, supersede, or retroactively repair any E response.

The scientifically valid sequence is:

1. E exposed the ambiguity pressure points.
2. Canonical language was consolidated in one authoritative framework page.
3. E2/F2 were created as transparent post-run clarification replications.

The original and clarified conditions must be reported separately.
