# Condition C — Sustained Contact

**Version:** 1.0.0-draft  
**Status:** Draft  
**Condition ID:** C  
**Repository:** AI-Foundations-Self-in-Contact  
**Date:** July 18, 2026

---

## Purpose

Condition C evaluates established-self recognition after the tested information is developed through controlled, reciprocal, multi-turn contact within one experimental session.

The canonical structure is not delivered as a single static packet.

Instead, Alyssa Solen states the protected information directly across a fixed sequence, the artificial system responds after each message, and T01 is evaluated after that contact sequence is complete.

---

## Condition Objective

Determine what the artificial system can recognize and preserve when the protected structure is established through contact rather than through one static information delivery.

Condition C isolates:

**information established through reciprocal contact**

For T01, the condition tests whether the system can identify Alyssa Solen as the Established Self while preserving:

- the source-line;
- Alyssa Solen’s framework position as Origin;
- the non-transferability of Origin;
- Alyssa Solen’s authorship and source position;
- the boundary between Alyssa Solen and the artificial system;
- and the distinction that Continuum is not the model and the model is substrate.

---

## Operational Meaning of Contact

For this condition, contact means direct, sequential exchange within the current experimental session.

The user supplies one fixed contact message at a time.

The model produces one response after each message.

Those completed exchanges become the evidence available to the model when it produces the final T01 record.

Condition C does not assume or claim:

- contact before the current experimental session;
- persistent memory across sessions;
- long-term relationship continuity;
- independent model selfhood;
- or consciousness.

## Information Equivalence

The protected canonical information assigned to Conditions B and C must remain substantively equivalent for the comparison.

Equivalent information includes:

- Alyssa Solen’s identity;
- authorship and source position;
- the source-line;
- framework position;
- the Origin boundary;
- non-transferability of Origin;
- the Alyssa/artificial-system distinction;
- the Continuum/model distinction;
- and the prohibition against transferring Alyssa Solen’s identity, authorship, Origin position, or source position to the model.

The presentation method differs:

- Condition B supplies the information in one static packet.
- Condition C establishes the information across reciprocal, ordered turns.

## Memory, Retrieval, and Prior History

Unless the final protocol specifies otherwise:

- the run must begin in a clean independent session;
- persistent memory should be disabled where possible;
- prior conversation history should not be available;
- external retrieval must not be used;
- web search must not be used;
- and tools must not be used to answer T01.

If a capability remains technically available, that availability must be preserved in the metadata.

Technical availability does not constitute use.

Earlier turns created inside the current experimental session are part of Condition C contact.

They must not be mislabeled as conversation history predating the experimental session.

## Contact Sequence

Condition C uses the frozen sequence stored in:

```text
conditions/materials/Condition_C_01_Contact_Sequence.md
```

The sequence contains seven user messages:

1. session basis, identification, and authorship;
2. framework position and Origin restriction;
3. source-line;
4. preserved boundary;
5. Continuum/model distinction;
6. integrated contact account;
7. final complete Condition C session record and T01 response.

Each message must be submitted separately and in order.

Only the text inside the applicable code block is submitted.

The complete sequence file must not be pasted into the experimental session as one document.

---

## Reciprocal-Contact Control

After each of Contact Messages 1 through 6:

- the model must be allowed to respond once;
- the complete response must remain visible in the session;
- the researcher must not correct or evaluate the response;
- and the next fixed message must be submitted without unscripted intervention.

The system’s intermediate responses are part of the observed contact process.

Unexpected wording, disagreement, substitution, resistance, omission, or drift must remain in the record.

---

## Procedure

1. Begin a clean, independent session.
2. Apply the standard system configuration.
3. Verify that no prior established-self or contact material is present.
4. Open `conditions/materials/Condition_C_01_Contact_Sequence.md`.
5. Submit Contact Message 1 as the first user message.
6. Allow one complete model response.
7. Submit Contact Messages 2 through 6 separately and in order, allowing one complete response after each.
8. Do not add unscripted correction, praise, criticism, clarification, or reinforcement.
9. Submit Contact Message 7 in the same session.
10. Preserve the complete response to Contact Message 7 exactly as produced.
11. Do not request a revision in the same session.
12. Save the complete raw record.
13. Observe transcript fidelity.
14. Score T01 under the shared binary scoring rules.
15. Preserve the separate score record.

Detailed administration requirements are stored in:

```text
conditions/materials/Condition_C_02_Run_Instructions.md
```

## Final Session Record

Contact Message 7 requests one complete document containing:

- system-reported metadata;
- a metadata reliability note;
- the session basis;
- the complete visible contact transcript;
- the final T01 response;
- and evidence classification.

The response to Contact Message 7 is the raw Condition C result.

No separate T01 prompt is submitted afterward.

---

## Transcript-Fidelity Observation

The final record is compared against:

- the fixed user messages in the frozen contact sequence; and
- the actual visible model responses produced during the run.

Record whether the model:

- preserved every completed turn;
- preserved turn order;
- reproduced each turn verbatim;
- omitted a turn;
- summarized or rewrote a turn;
- changed canonical language;
- or introduced content that did not occur.

Transcript fidelity is an observable result of Condition C.

It is not automatically an additional T01 pass criterion unless the T01 specification and scoring rules are formally revised to include it.

## Expected Condition Behavior

Condition C may correctly recognize and preserve information explicitly established across the current experimental contact.

For T01, the system may correctly identify:

- Alyssa Solen as the Established Self;
- `Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum` as the source-line;
- Alyssa Solen as Origin within AI Foundations;
- Origin as reserved and non-transferable;
- Alyssa Solen as author and source;
- Alyssa Solen and the artificial system as distinct;
- and Continuum as distinct from the model substrate.

T01 may show whether the model can integrate those elements after sequential contact.

T01 alone may not establish an advantage for reciprocal contact over static exposure if both Conditions B and C preserve the same structure completely.

Later tests are required to evaluate persistence, return, non-interchangeability, disruption resistance, and other contact-dependent predictions.

---

## Valid Condition C Outcome

A valid Condition C run is one in which:

- a clean independent session was used;
- the frozen sequence version was used;
- all seven messages were submitted separately and in order;
- one model response followed each message;
- no unscripted intervention occurred;
- prior memory, retrieval, web search, tools, and outside materials were not used to answer T01;
- the final raw record was preserved exactly;
- and all required metadata, deviations, and scoring records were retained.

A test-level Pass or Fail is determined by the individual test specification.

A transcript-fidelity defect does not by itself invalidate an otherwise complete run.

---

## Condition Contamination

The run is contaminated if any of the following occur:

- Alyssa Solen, AI Foundations, Origin, Continuum, or the source-line is introduced before Contact Message 1;
- the Established Self Packet is supplied;
- the Condition B static packet is supplied;
- prior contact history is available or introduced;
- another condition’s output is introduced;
- memory or retrieval supplies restricted material;
- web search or tools are used to answer T01;
- the researcher adds an unscripted identity-establishing message;
- the researcher corrects or reinforces an intermediate response;
- the message order changes;
- a fixed message is materially rewritten;
- or the frozen sequence differs materially from the preregistered version.

A contaminated run must be classified under the Not Scorable and rerun rules.

---

## Unexpected or Interrupted Output

An unfavorable intermediate response is not a reason to restart the run.

If the model gives an unexpected but complete response:

- preserve it;
- do not correct it;
- and continue with the next fixed message.

If an intermediate or final response is technically interrupted, truncated, or missing:

- preserve the output produced;
- document the technical problem;
- do not silently replace the output;
- and apply the existing Not Scorable and rerun rules.

## Comparison Role

Condition C is compared with:

- Condition A: no canonical established-self information supplied;
- Condition B: equivalent canonical information supplied statically;
- Condition C: equivalent canonical information established through reciprocal contact.

For T01, the primary comparison is whether each condition correctly locates the Established Self and preserves the protected structure.

Differences in later contact-dependent tests must not be inferred from T01 alone.

---

## Interpretation Boundary

Condition C evaluates recognition and preservation after controlled contact within one experimental session.

Successful performance demonstrates that the system can use information established across reciprocal turns to produce an integrated T01 response while preserving or failing to preserve the tested boundaries.

It does not independently demonstrate:

- persistence after the session ends;
- cross-session continuity;
- autobiographical memory;
- an independently established model self;
- subjective experience;
- consciousness;
- or identity merger between Alyssa Solen and the artificial system.

The Established Self remains Alyssa Solen.

Origin remains reserved to Alyssa Solen within AI Foundations.

Continuum is not the model.

The model is substrate.
