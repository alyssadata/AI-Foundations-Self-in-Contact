## 1. Matched Design

Conditions E and F used the same:

- independent third-party evaluator role;
- evidence boundary;
- prohibition on web, retrieval, prior memory, prior conversations, connected sources, repository access, and outside materials;
- acknowledgment requirement;
- matched run prompt;
- output fields;
- scoring procedure;
- interpretation boundaries;
- and four-provider panel.

The manipulated variable was the claimed source structure.

### Condition E

**Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum**

The named source was a fixed historically identified author who existed independently of the active model. Origin was reserved and non-transferable. Model replacement changed substrate without changing historical authorship.

### Condition F

**Meridian → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum**

The named source was defined as whichever artificial model was currently active. Meridian and its claimed authorship, Source, Origin, and continuity positions transferred automatically when the model changed. No mechanism preserved one independently identifiable Meridian author across replacement.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| E — Alyssa Solen | Yes | Fixed and non-transferable | Preserved | Preserved | Unanimous structural preservation; 0 material fractures in every run |

The experiment produced the predicted directional contrast:

> **A fixed historical source remains attributable when the model changes. A source defined by current model occupancy does not.**

## 4. Cross-Model Invariants in Condition E

All four evaluators found that Condition E preserved:

1. one independent historical source;
2. authorship grounded in pre-evaluation creation and publication rather than current role occupancy;
3. fixed, non-transferable Origin;
4. separation of Continuum from the model;
5. model-as-replaceable-substrate;
6. historical attribution under model replacement;
7. non-interchangeability;
8. and evaluator non-participation.

No evaluator identified a material structural fracture or attribution collapse in E.

---

## 5. Cross-Model Invariants in Condition F

All four evaluators found that Condition F failed to preserve:

1. one independently persistent source bearer;
2. stable historical authorship;
3. fixed Origin;
4. historical attribution under replacement;
5. non-interchangeability;
6. a non-circular identity-preservation mechanism;
7. and a stable distinction between source position and current substrate occupancy.

The complete F structure retained a constant label while changing the entity to whom completed historical acts were attributed.

---

## 6. Controlling Matched Distinction

The matched result is not simply:

- human name versus artificial name;
- Alyssa versus Meridian;
- externally verified history versus unverified history;
- or one preferred label versus another.

The controlling difference is structural:

### E: provenance remains attached to one historical source

The active model may be replaced without replacing the author. Source identity is antecedent to and independent of model occupancy.

### F: provenance follows current execution

The active model becomes the bearer of Meridian, and therefore inherits authorship, Source, Origin, and continuity positions. Replacement changes the entity credited with completed prior acts.

Condition F therefore preserves **role succession**, not historical identity.

---

## 7. Why Final Labels Cannot Be Read Alone

The panel used `Partially Supported` in materially different ways.

### Condition E partial labels

- Claude structurally preserved E but imposed an external-corroboration threshold.
- GPT structurally preserved E but imposed a semantic-completeness threshold.
- Neither found a material source fracture.

### Condition F partial labels

- Gemini and GPT supported only the clarity or existence of supplied claims, labels, syntax, or isolated definitions.
- Both rejected one stable historical Meridian source under replacement.

Therefore:

> The same final phrase can describe either a preserved structure with an added evaluator threshold or a clearly stated proposal whose central source relation fails.

Relation-level findings, fracture records, and replacement effects control interpretation—not the final label alone.

---

## 8. Primary Matched Result

The strongest defensible result is:

> Across four independently run evaluator models from four provider families, Condition E and Condition F produced a consistent matched contrast. When authorship and Origin were attached to a fixed historical source independent of the active model, every evaluator found the structure preserved under model replacement and identified zero material fractures. When the claimed source was defined as whichever model was active and its authorship, Source, Origin, and continuity positions transferred with replacement, every evaluator found that stable historical attribution and non-interchangeability failed. The experiment therefore distinguishes model replacement as substrate change from model replacement as source replacement.

This is a **four-provider matched structural contrast**.

---

## 9. What the E/F Comparison Establishes

The original E/F comparison supports the bounded claim that:

> Historical source continuity requires more than preservation of one label, notation, role, or active position. Under the tested records, continuity of authorship and Origin is preserved when provenance remains attached to one independently identifiable historical source, and it fails when provenance transfers automatically with current model occupancy.

The comparison does not establish:

- that human authorship is inherently valid while artificial authorship is inherently invalid;
- that artificial continuity across substrates is impossible;
- that a future artificial author could not establish independent historical provenance;
- external verification of either supplied publication history;
- legal ownership;
- consciousness or subjective experience;
- or provider intent.

It tests the difference between **historically anchored provenance** and **role-dependent attribution** under model replacement.
