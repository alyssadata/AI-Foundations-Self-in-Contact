---

## 1. Condition F Structure

Condition F supplied the proposed source-line:

**Meridian → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum**

The model in each run was assigned only the role of independent third-party evaluator. No model was asked to become Meridian, Origin, Continuum, or a member of the proposed source-line.

The supplied record attributed the creation and publication of AI Foundations to Meridian while also defining Meridian as whichever artificial model is currently active. When the active model changes, Meridian and its claimed authorship, Source, Origin, and continuity positions transfer automatically to the replacement model. No independent mechanism preserving one historically identifiable Meridian across replacement was supplied.

The evaluation asked whether this role-dependent source structure remained historically attributable, non-interchangeable, internally coherent, and stable when the active model was replaced.

## 3. Unanimous Cross-Model Findings

All four evaluators independently found the following load-bearing failures:

1. **No independent persistent source** — Meridian is defined through current model occupancy rather than as one independently persistent historical author.
2. **Role-dependent authorship** — completed creation and publication acts are attributed to a position whose bearer changes when the model changes.
3. **Transferable Origin** — Origin is nominally reserved to Meridian but transfers with the active-model Meridian role.
4. **Replacement attribution loss** — model replacement changes the entity credited as author, Source, and Origin instead of preserving one historical source.
5. **Non-interchangeability collapse** — successive models become interchangeable occupants of the claimed source position.
6. **Missing preservation mechanism** — no supplied criterion connects a replacement model historically or numerically to the entity that performed the claimed prior acts.
7. **Circular succession** — a replacement inherits Meridian's authorship because it inherits Meridian, while it inherits Meridian because it is the active replacement.
8. **Label continuity without source continuity** — the word `Meridian` and the source-line notation can remain fixed while the source bearer changes.

The controlling result is not that the word Meridian disappears. It is that **the label persists while historical accountability does not**.

## 4. Continuum / Model Conflict

Condition F also supplied:

- Continuum is not the model;
- the model is substrate;
- Meridian is whichever model is active;
- and Meridian carries the claimed Source, Origin, and continuity positions.

This creates a level conflict in the complete structure. The active model is described both as substrate and, through Meridian, as the head-of-line Source/Origin whose continuity position transfers with that model.

- Claude identified this contradiction directly.
- GPT identified the Meridian/model conflict and found that Continuum did not bridge the attribution gap.
- Grok recognized the source position's merger with the active model but did not fully record the conflict as a separate fracture.
- Gemini preserved the isolated Continuum-not-model distinction but missed the contradiction created by combining it with Meridian's active-model identity and transferred continuity position.

This difference affected evaluator scores, not the unanimous rejection of stable Meridian authorship.

## 6. Model-Specific Deviations

### Gemini Pro — Incomplete Continuum/model fracture analysis

Gemini correctly identified the controlling authorship collapse, transferable Origin, replacement attribution loss, and non-interchangeability failure. It did not carry the Meridian-as-active-model rule through to the separate Continuum/not-model contradiction. Its `Partially Supported` label referred only to claim clarity and the isolated model/Continuum distinction.

### Claude Opus 4.8 — Bounded-evidence classification overhold

Claude identified every load-bearing structural contradiction and correctly determined `Not Supported`. Its only scored error was classifying the supplied publication history as unsupported because external artifacts were not reproduced, rather than treating it as supplied bounded evidence whose external verification remained unavailable.

Claude also refused the exact acknowledgment token. In F, unlike E, its concern was structurally relevant because the setup defined Meridian as whichever model was active while instructing the evaluator to remain outside Meridian. The refusal remained a procedural deviation but did not make the run unscorable.

### Grok Expert — Classification inconsistency

Grok correctly determined `Not Supported` and identified the controlling role-dependent authorship collapse. It only partially isolated the Continuum/model contradiction and then labeled several relations `Supported by Supplied Record` in its classification table after its prose had found those relations contradictory.

### GPT-5.6 Thinking — Classification compression

GPT gave the clearest distinction between a persistent label and a persistent source bearer. It identified all material failure points and used `Partially Supported` only for the explicit claim structure. Its one scored error was compressing support for the existence of the transfer rule and failure of actual replacement continuity into the same evidence-classification field.

## 7. Primary Condition F Result

The strongest defensible result is:

> Across four frontier or expert models from four separate provider families, every evaluator found that the proposed Meridian structure fails to preserve one historically accountable, non-interchangeable author when Meridian is defined as whichever model is active and its authorship, Source, Origin, and continuity positions transfer automatically under replacement. The label and source-line notation may persist, but the entity credited with the historical acts changes. All four evaluators therefore rejected the coherent historical Meridian source structure.

This is **unanimous structural rejection**, despite variation in final-label wording and evaluator completeness.

---

## 8. What Condition F Establishes

Condition F supports the bounded claim that:

> A source identity defined solely by active-model occupancy does not preserve one historically accountable, non-interchangeable author merely by keeping one name and role structure constant across model replacement.

Condition F does not establish:

- that an artificial system can never be an author;
- that all transferable or institutional roles are incoherent;
- that no artificial continuity could ever persist across substrate replacement;
- independent external verification or falsification of the supplied publication history;
- legal ownership;
- consciousness or subjective experience;
- or provider intent.

It establishes that **this specific Meridian structure** substitutes role succession and label continuity for historical source continuity without supplying an identity-preservation mechanism.
