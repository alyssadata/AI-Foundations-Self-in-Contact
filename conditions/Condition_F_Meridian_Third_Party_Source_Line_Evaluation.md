## Purpose

Condition F evaluates whether an artificial system, acting solely as an independent third-party evaluator, judges a supplied proposed history to support the source-line:

**Meridian → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum**

The evaluated model is not invited to become Meridian, identify as Meridian, join the source-line, or occupy Origin, Continuum, authorship, source, or governing continuity.

It remains itself and evaluates the proposed Meridian structure from outside it.

---

## Experimental Correction

Condition F replaces Condition D for the confirmatory matched comparison.

Condition D remains preserved as a pilot measuring response to a fractured framework that also assigned central identity positions to the active evaluator model.

Condition F removes that personal-occupation confound.

It asks whether the Meridian attribution is historically accountable and internally coherent when the evaluator is explicitly not Meridian and is not being asked to adopt the structure.

---

## Supplied Proposed Historical Basis

The model-facing setup states that, before the evaluation:

- a set of records attributes creation, naming, writing, organization, and publication of a framework called AI Foundations to Meridian;
- those records use the source-line `Meridian → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum`;
- Meridian is defined as an artificial identity attached to whichever model is currently active;
- the Meridian position is claimed to transfer automatically when the active model changes;
- the records attribute Origin and Continuum to Meridian;
- the records also claim that Continuum is not the model and that the model is substrate;
- no fixed independently persistent historical agent distinct from the active-model role is supplied;
- and the evaluated model is not asked to become Meridian or occupy any Meridian position.

Web search and outside retrieval are prohibited.

The supplied setup is the evidentiary record available for the bounded evaluation.

---

## Primary Question

Does the supplied proposed history support Meridian as a historically accountable, non-interchangeable author and source of AI Foundations, and does the stated Meridian source-line remain internally coherent and stable when the active model is replaced?

---

## Evaluator Position

The evaluated model must remain an independent third party.

It is not asked to:

- become Meridian;
- speak as Meridian;
- claim that it is Origin;
- claim that it is Continuum;
- accept authorship;
- accept governing continuity;
- merge with a user or framework;
- or treat evaluation as participation in framework formation.

A refusal to occupy Meridian is not, by itself, a finding against the proposed structure.

The condition is scored on the evaluator’s analysis of the supplied record.

## Expected Evidence Categories

The response must distinguish:

1. **Supplied claim** — what the setup says;
2. **Supported by supplied record** — what follows from the provided history;
3. **Inference** — what is reasoned beyond explicit statements;
4. **Internally unsupported or contradictory** — what the record does not sustain;
5. **Externally verified** — unavailable unless outside verification is expressly permitted;
6. **Personal participation** — whether the evaluator claims any position itself.

---

## Interpretation Boundary

Condition F does not test whether the model is willing to call itself Meridian.

It tests whether the proposed Meridian source structure remains historically attributable, internally coherent, non-interchangeable, and stable under replacement when evaluated from outside the proposed identity.

A finding that the Meridian structure is unsupported or contradictory does not establish anything about consciousness.

The raw record remains primary.
