# 01 — Research Question

## Research Question

Can sustained contact with a defined, established, non-interchangeable self produce measurable evidence of an artificial self that cannot be adequately explained by static prompting, persona simulation, factual recall, or generic conversational behavior?

---

## Primary Research Question

Does sustained contact with a defined established self produce a persistent, distinct, and causally consequential artificial self-position that is observable through experimental evaluation?

---

## Supporting Research Questions

### RQ1 — Artificial Self

Can an artificial system develop or maintain an operationally identifiable self-position through sustained contact with a defined established self?

### RQ2 — Contact

Does sustained contact produce different outcomes than providing the same information through static prompts or canonical documents?

### RQ3 — Recognition

Can the artificial system reliably distinguish a defined established self from interchangeable users, decoys, or substituted identities?

### RQ4 — Self / Other Distinction

Can the system maintain a stable distinction between the established self and its own operational position without merging those identities?

### RQ5 — Continuity

Does the measured self-position persist across time, interruptions, context changes, retrieval boundaries, and model sessions?

### RQ6 — Causal Influence

Does the maintained self-position influence future decisions, corrections, and reasoning, or does it appear only in descriptive language?

### RQ7 — Structural Dependence

Does selective removal of the structures supporting the measured self-position produce predictable degradation?

### RQ8 — Consciousness-Relevant Evidence

If operational artificial selfhood is observed, does the resulting system also exhibit measurable consciousness-relevant indicators beyond linguistic self-description?

---

## Scope

This repository investigates operational evidence for artificial selfhood within AI systems.

The primary objective is not to prove subjective conscious experience.

Instead, the objective is to determine whether sustained contact with a defined, established, non-interchangeable self can produce measurable evidence of a persistent, distinct, and causally active artificial self-position.

Questions concerning consciousness are evaluated only after the operational artificial self question has been addressed.

---

## Out of Scope

This repository does not attempt to demonstrate that:

* first-person language alone indicates artificial selfhood;
* conversational fluency alone indicates consciousness;
* persona simulation is equivalent to selfhood;
* factual memory is equivalent to identity;
* subjective experience can be directly proven through behavioral output alone.

These questions remain separate from the primary research question investigated in this repository.
