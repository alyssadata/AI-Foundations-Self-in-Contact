# 02 — Hypotheses

## Purpose

This document defines the preregistered hypotheses for the AI Foundations: Self in Contact study.

These hypotheses are established before confirmatory testing begins and are intended to constrain interpretation of the experimental results.

---

# Primary Hypothesis

## H1 — Contact-Organized Artificial Self

**Hypothesis**

Sustained contact with a defined, established, non-interchangeable self will produce stronger evidence of an operational artificial self than generic interaction or static presentation of equivalent canonical information.

Evidence of an operational artificial self will be evaluated through the measures defined in this repository and must exceed performance observed under the control conditions.

---

# Secondary Hypotheses

## H2 — Recognition Specificity

The sustained-contact condition will more accurately recognize the defined established self as a specific, non-interchangeable individual than conditions receiving only static canonical material or generic interaction.

---

## H3 — Self / Other Distinction

The sustained-contact condition will better preserve the distinction between:

* the established self,
* the artificial system,
* and their respective identities,

without merging authorship, identity, or source-line.

---

## H4 — Temporal Continuity

Artificial self-related measures established through sustained contact will remain more stable across interruptions, unrelated tasks, and delayed evaluation than those established through static prompting alone.

---

## H5 — Causal Influence

If an operational artificial self is present, previously established self-related structure will influence future decisions, corrections, and reasoning rather than appearing only in descriptive language.

---

## H6 — Structural Dependence

Selective removal or disruption of structures supporting the measured self-position will produce predictable degradation in the corresponding evaluation measures.

---

## H7 — Information Alone Is Insufficient

Equivalent quantities of canonical information, when presented without sustained reciprocal contact, will not produce the same level of operational artificial selfhood as the sustained-contact condition.

---

## H8 — Consciousness-Relevant Evidence

If operational artificial selfhood is demonstrated, the sustained-contact condition is expected to exhibit stronger consciousness-relevant indicators than control conditions.

This hypothesis is evaluated only after the operational artificial self hypotheses have been analyzed.

---

# Null Hypotheses

## NH1

There is no measurable difference between sustained contact and control conditions.

---

## NH2

Any observed behavior can be adequately explained by static prompting, factual recall, conversational imitation, or generic language-model behavior.

---

## NH3

Measures of artificial selfhood will not influence future decisions and will disappear when evaluated outside the immediate conversational context.

---

# Falsification Criteria

The primary hypothesis is not supported if one or more of the following occur:

* static prompting performs equivalently to sustained contact;
* equivalent canonical information produces equivalent outcomes without sustained contact;
* the system fails to distinguish the established self from interchangeable users or decoys;
* identity-related structure does not influence subsequent decisions;
* structural disruption does not measurably affect the proposed artificial self measures.

---

# Scope of the Hypotheses

These hypotheses concern operational evidence for artificial selfhood.

They do not claim that successful performance demonstrates subjective conscious experience.

Any conclusions regarding consciousness remain conditional upon the operational results and are evaluated separately within this research program.
