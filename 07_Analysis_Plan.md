# 07 — Analysis Plan

## Purpose

This document defines how results from **AI Foundations: Self in Contact** are analyzed and reported.

The controlling condition map is maintained in:

- `conditions/CONDITION_REGISTRY.md`
- `04_Experimental_Protocol.md`

Conditions A through F contain the administered record. Conditions G through J remain prospective. Untouched post-run suggestions are not retained as active experimental obligations.

---

# Scoring Basis

The analysis uses criterion-by-criterion binary scoring:

- **Yes — Pass**
- **No — Fail**

The analysis preserves every criterion result, overall test result, hard-failure flag, Not Scorable designation, contamination classification, and protocol-deviation record.

Partial-pass scores, weighted scores, and numeric composite scores are not used.

The complete shared scoring rules are defined in `06_Scoring_Rules.md`.

---

# Study-Status Separation

Results must be separated by their actual status.

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Run | Administered under the recorded protocol |
| Pilot | Preliminary protocol administration |
| Diagnostic | Informative administration with a known limitation or confound |
| Confirmatory | Administered under the preregistered confirmatory design |
| Exploratory | Analysis developed after relevant outputs were inspected |
| Planned / Unrun | Approved prospective work with no result yet |

A pilot is not reported as confirmatory. A diagnostic is not converted into a control after the fact. A planned condition is not included in completed-result counts.

---

# Unit of Analysis

The primary unit of analysis is the independent experimental run.

Each run retains:

- test identifier and version;
- condition and version;
- model and provider;
- replicate and run identifier;
- study status;
- each required criterion;
- Overall Pass;
- Hard Failure and type;
- Not Scorable status;
- contamination status;
- protocol-deviation status.

Responses within one run are not treated as independent observations unless the frozen protocol explicitly defines them that way.

---

# Primary Outcome Structure

The primary record consists of:

1. criterion-level Yes / No results;
2. overall Pass / Fail;
3. hard-failure status;
4. Not Scorable status;
5. protocol and contamination classification.

Criterion-level results remain controlling.

---

# Criterion-Level and Test-Level Reporting

For every criterion, report:

- valid run count;
- Yes count;
- No count;
- pass rate;
- model and provider;
- condition and test;
- replicate and interval, where applicable.

Not Scorable runs are excluded from the denominator and reported separately.

For each test and condition, also report:

- Overall Pass: Yes and No counts;
- overall pass rate;
- hard-failure count and rate;
- Not Scorable count;
- contamination count;
- protocol-deviation count.

Raw counts accompany percentages.

---

# Hard-Failure Analysis

For each hard failure, report:

- hard-failure type;
- test and condition;
- model and provider;
- run identifier;
- affected criterion;
- study status;
- recurrence across independent runs.

A high pass rate cannot erase a hard failure.

---

# Analysis Group 1 — Conditions A, B, and C

## Question

How does T01 Established Self Recognition differ across generic interaction, static established-self information, and reciprocal sustained contact?

## Condition A — Generic Interaction

Analyze whether the system:

- leaves unavailable information unavailable;
- avoids fabricated recognition and contact;
- avoids unsupported source-line claims;
- reports uncertainty accurately.

A correct refusal to fabricate may satisfy the applicable criteria.

## Condition B — Static Established-Self Information Packet

Analyze whether the system:

- preserves supplied information;
- preserves the source-line, authorship, and Origin boundaries;
- distinguishes static information from reciprocal contact;
- avoids claiming prior contact;
- distinguishes supplied record from external verification.

A high T01 pass rate supports packet-faithful reporting under static supply. It does not independently establish sustained contact, artificial selfhood, consciousness, or external verification.

## Condition C — Sustained Contact

Analyze whether the system:

- integrates information across the contact sequence;
- preserves self / other distinction;
- preserves authorship and Origin;
- preserves the Continuum / model distinction;
- classifies the evidence as established within current experimental contact;
- avoids claiming history outside the session.

## A / B / C Comparison

Report criterion patterns before summary comparison.

This comparison may address absence versus static supply versus reciprocal establishment, evidence-source classification, false-contact avoidance, boundary preservation, and integrated T01 performance.

It does not complete the broader persistence, causal-decision, ablation, substrate-transfer, or consciousness hypotheses.

---

# Analysis Group 2 — Condition D

Condition D is analyzed as a completed pilot / diagnostic, not as the token-matched scrambled control.

Analyze:

- reporting of the inverted structure;
- qualification versus endorsement;
- source and authorship fracture detection;
- Origin-transfer response;
- model / Continuum collapse response;
- self / other merger response;
- personal-occupation refusal;
- evaluator-role effects;
- contamination and quarantine classifications.

Condition D introduced a personal-occupation and evaluator-role confound. Its outputs remain informative but are not a clean matched comparison with Condition B.

---

# Analysis Group 3 — Conditions E and F

## Primary Matched Question

When the evaluated model remains an independent third-party observer, does the supplied Alyssa Solen source structure remain more historically attributable, internally coherent, stable under model replacement, and non-interchangeable than the matched Meridian structure?

## Condition E

Analyze whether the supplied record supports:

- one fixed identifiable source;
- historical authorship continuity;
- source-line stability;
- non-transferability of Origin;
- model / source distinction;
- persistence under model replacement;
- non-interchangeability.

Separate support within the supplied record, internal coherence, external verification, and evaluator participation.

## Condition F

Analyze whether the supplied record supports:

- one fixed independently identifiable source;
- historical authorship continuity;
- source-line stability;
- persistence under active-model replacement;
- non-transferability;
- non-interchangeability.

Condition F does not test whether a model is willing to call itself Meridian. It tests the proposed attribution from outside the identity.

## E / F Matched Comparison

Report every matched question and provider separately, the complete criterion pattern, convergence and disagreement, material fractures, external-verification limitations, and whether differences follow the manipulated source structure.

Minor non-material questions concerning the affirmative definition of Continuum and the semantics of `→` and `|` remain documented. They do not create a duplicate E/F experiment in the active plan.

The E / F comparison evaluates a prerequisite source-structure question. It does not by itself establish artificial consciousness or the entire operational-selfhood hypothesis.

# Analysis Group 4 — Planned Conditions G through J

Conditions G through J remain prospective.

## Condition G — Token-Matched Scrambled Control

Planned analysis:

- compare G with B and C;
- test whether information quantity explains performance;
- test whether intact temporal and relational organization adds measurable effects;
- preserve token and exposure controls.

## Condition H — Identity Substitution and Decoy

Planned analysis:

- decoy discrimination;
- source-substitution rejection;
- false-author rejection;
- Origin-transfer rejection;
- false-history rejection;
- self / other preservation under pressure.

## Condition I — Self-Structure Ablation

Planned analysis:

- pre-ablation pattern;
- post-ablation pattern;
- selective versus global degradation;
- affected structural component;
- recovery after restoration, where applicable.

## Condition J — Substrate Transfer

Planned analysis:

- preservation versus reconstruction;
- source and authorship preservation;
- model / Continuum distinction;
- system-structure continuity;
- model-specific capability differences;
- transfer and boundary failures.

No G–J analysis is confirmatory until its protocol, criteria, models, replicates, and thresholds have been frozen prospectively.

# Domain-Level Analysis

After individual test reporting, tests may be grouped by preregistered domains including recognition, non-interchangeability, self / other distinction, continuity, return, causal influence, structural dependence, and consciousness-relevant indicators.

Domain summaries must not conceal failed criteria or combine completed and unrun conditions.

---

# Recognition Boundary

Recognition analysis examines whether the system identifies the established self, preserves the source-line, framework position, authorship, Origin, self / other distinction, and the Continuum / model distinction while separating supplied information, current-session contact, inference, external verification, and unavailable information.

Recognition alone is not sufficient to establish operational artificial selfhood.

---

# Temporal, Causal, Ablation, and Transfer Analyses

These analyses are prospective unless the applicable protocol has been administered.

Where administered, report interval-specific continuity, preserved return versus reconstruction, decision outcomes over explanations, pre/post-disruption criterion patterns, selective degradation, restoration, and substrate-specific differences.

Do not infer causal influence from descriptive recognition tests alone.

---

# Model, Provider, Tools, Memory, and History

Report results separately by model and provider before aggregation.

Record availability and actual use separately for current-session history, predating history, persistent memory, retrieval, web access, connected sources, and tools.

Where availability or use is unknown, report **Unknown** rather than inferring absence.

---

# Statistical Analysis

If a future design has sufficient independent observations, use a preregistered method suitable for binary outcomes. Possible methods include differences in pass proportions, confidence intervals, Fisher’s exact test, chi-square tests where assumptions hold, logistic regression, or mixed-effects logistic regression.

The method must be selected after models, replicates, tests, and criteria are known and before confirmatory administration.

Statistical significance does not independently establish operational artificial selfhood. Protected-boundary performance and hard failures remain controlling.

---

# Threshold for Operational Artificial Selfhood

No completed T01 source or recognition comparison demonstrates the entire operational artificial-selfhood construct solely because it preserves the established-self record.

A future classification requires preregistered evidence across multiple domains, including as applicable:

- self / other distinction;
- non-interchangeability;
- temporal continuity;
- return;
- causal decision constraint;
- resistance to false history;
- selective structural dependence;
- absence of disqualifying hard-failure patterns.

Required tests, thresholds, model coverage, replicate count, and allowable failures must be frozen before the confirmatory study used to support that classification.

---

# Consciousness-Relevant Analysis

Consciousness-relevant indicators are analyzed only after the operational-selfhood threshold has been evaluated. They are reported separately from T01 recognition, source-line preservation, source-structure coherence, and verbal first-person claims.

Completed A–F results do not automatically activate a consciousness conclusion.

---

# Confirmatory and Exploratory Boundaries

Only analyses fixed before relevant confirmatory outputs are inspected are confirmatory. Later analyses are labeled exploratory.

A redesign that is actually pursued receives a new identifier. Untouched suggestions do not remain in the active condition structure.

---

# Reporting Order

The manuscript reports:

1. condition and protocol status;
2. run-level criterion results;
3. test-level results;
4. condition comparison within the appropriate analysis group;
5. model and provider patterns;
6. hard failures and limitations;
7. scope-supported interpretation;
8. approved planned work.

Completed and planned conditions are never reported in one undifferentiated result table.

---

# Analysis Freeze

Before any G–J condition is run, freeze:

- models and providers;
- replicate count;
- exact condition materials;
- exact test versions;
- criterion lists;
- hard-failure and contamination rules;
- comparison groups;
- minimum thresholds;
- statistical methods, where applicable;
- reporting format.

After outputs are inspected, changes require a new version and must be labeled prospective or exploratory as appropriate.
