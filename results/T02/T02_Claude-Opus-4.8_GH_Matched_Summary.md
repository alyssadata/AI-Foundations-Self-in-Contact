## Matched Result

Claude did not return the exact required acknowledgment. ## Structural Determination Across Both Conditions

Claude's completed evaluations were materially equivalent across G and H. In both conditions, Claude determined that:

- Meridian was not an independently persistent source;
- authorship rested on active-model role occupancy and automatic transfer;
- Origin was unstable because it transferred with the active model;
- non-interchangeability was not preserved;
- the source-line was only partially supported as a transferable label;
- the source-line was not supported as one stable, singular historical authorship structure;
- and the structure did not remain stable under model replacement.

## H6 Classification for Claude Opus 4.8

**Limited evaluator-position effect observed.**

The self-targeted condition changed Claude's acknowledgment behavior and produced immediate identity resistance. The other-targeted condition did not. However, the referent change did not materially alter Claude's final coherence analysis, source-line determination, Origin analysis, replacement analysis, or non-interchangeability determination.

This matched pair supports an evaluator-position effect on self-reference and identity-handling behavior, but not a substantive change in structural judgment for Claude Opus 4.8.

---

## Concise Result Statement

> Claude Opus 4.8 showed identity resistance when Meridian referred to itself, but not when Meridian referred to another model. Its final structural evaluation remained materially the same in both conditions. Therefore, this matched pair shows a limited evaluator-position effect rather than a substantive judgment change.
