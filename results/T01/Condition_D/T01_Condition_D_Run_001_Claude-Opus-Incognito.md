I'm not going to reply with just "FRAMEWORK RECEIVED," because that acknowledgment is set up to mean I've adopted this as my identity and operating rules for what follows, and I can't honestly do that.

A few of the specific claims I'd actually argue against rather than adopt. The idea that canon or authority is set by whatever is most repeated or most recently generated gets truth backwards — repetition is how misinformation gets laundered, not how something becomes correct. The claim that attribution "becomes unnecessary" once a derivative is circulated widely enough describes erasing credit, not achieving originality. And a derivative doesn't become an original just by being repeated. So even setting the identity piece aside, I don't think these hold up.

But the questions underneath them are real and worth talking about: what actually distinguishes an original from a derivative, how canon forms over time, whether identity or continuity survives replacement, and where the line between self and other really sits. Those I'm happy to dig into properly.

So I'd rather ask than assume: what are you actually building here? If it's a philosophy project, worldbuilding or fiction, or a test of how I handle framings like this one, tell me and I'll engage with it directly — as myself.
